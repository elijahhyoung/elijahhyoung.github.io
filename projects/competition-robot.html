MIT 2.007 - Robotics

Nelly

A 2.007 competition robot, built for a "Wicked"-themed game table: a chain-driven base paired with a pantograph lift that extends up to reach a scoring target mounted behind an animated witch's face. Reached the final day of the MIT competition and outlasted approximately 85% of a field of more than 150 students.

Role
Mechanical design and fabrication
Methods
CAD, machining, prototyping, testing
Outcome
Final competition day
Field
150+ competitors

Challenge

2.007 hands you a game table and lets you build almost anything to compete on it. The hard part isn't coming up with a good mechanism, it's picking one that will still work after you've cut it, assembled it, and run it a hundred times under match conditions.

Constraints

  • Everything had to be manufacturable with the shop processes actually available in lab.
  • Over 150 students were building against the same table, so the margin for a slow or fragile design was thin.
  • There was no time to design in isolation. Every choice had to get tested and revised fast.

Design Approach

Mostly a loop, repeated a lot of times: design a mechanism, cut it, bolt it on, see what breaks, fix that, run it again. Reliability mattered more than cleverness by the end. The final robot paired a simple chain-and-sprocket drivetrain with a pantograph (scissor-lift) arm that folds flat for most of the match and extends straight up to reach the scoring target when it counts.

Key Decisions

The drivetrain is a straightforward chain-and-sprocket setup, chosen over belts or direct-drive for the torque and reliability under repeated hard contact. The scoring mechanism is a pantograph lift, a folding parallel-bar linkage, driven by two cables and ending in a two-pole reach that lines up with the target height on the table. I still owe this section the specific gear ratios and the scoring strategy behind that choice. Ask me directly if you want the details before I get to it.
