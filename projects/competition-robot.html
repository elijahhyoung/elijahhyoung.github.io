MIT 2.007 - Robotics

Nelly

A 2.007 competition robot built for a "Wicked"-themed game table: two-wheel rear drive on a pair of speed motors, a 5-stage linear slide that telescopes from 15.5" up to over 57" to retrieve a broom hanging above the table, and a separate actuated claw on the base that grasps the broom and holds it while I drive it onto my side of the board.

Role
Mechanical design and fabrication
Outcome
16th / 150+
Size / weight
12"x12"x16", 4 lb 2 oz
Methods
CAD, machining, prototyping, testing

Challenge

2.007 hands you a game table and lets you build almost anything to compete on it. The hard part isn't coming up with a good mechanism, it's picking one that will still work after you've cut it, assembled it, and run it a hundred times under match conditions.

  • Had to be manufacturable with the shop processes available in lab.
  • 150+ students competed on the same table, leaving little margin for error.
  • No time to design in isolation; every choice got tested and revised fast.

Approach & Key Decisions

I targeted the broom because it was the single most valuable point object on the board and the most interesting engineering challenge: reaching it meant a mechanism that could extend far above the table and grab hold of it cleanly. Design followed a fast loop, build a mechanism, cut it, bolt it on, see what breaks, fix it, run it again, and the final robot kept the drivetrain deliberately simple, two wheels, rear drive, so the more complex reach mechanism had a stable base to work from.

Drive is two-wheel rear drive on a pair of 25-3 speed motors. Scoring uses two separate mechanisms on the base: a 5-stage linear slide on a pulley system, powered by a single 25-2 torque motor, that telescopes from a stowed 15.5" up to over 57" to retrieve the broom, and a separate actuated claw that then grasps the broom once it's back within reach.

Fabrication & Testing

Fabrication combined manual milling and lathe work, waterjetting, laser cutting, 3D printing, hand tools, and rapid prototyping to get from design to working parts quickly. Every mechanism went through repeated testing under real match conditions before competition, the video above is that match footage, the linear slide extending to reach the broom.

Results

Placed 16th in the competition, which secured a spot in the final day tournament in the MIT 2.007 competition.

Scored 150 points in head-to-head competition after successfully securing the broom, shown on the scoreboard below.
