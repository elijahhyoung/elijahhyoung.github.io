MIT 2.007 - Robotics

Competition Robot

Designed and fabricated a competition robot through mechanism design, machining, rapid prototyping, and iteration. The robot reached the final day of the MIT competition and outlasted approximately 85% of a field of more than 150 students.

Role
Mechanical design and fabrication
Methods
CAD, machining, prototyping, testing
Outcome
Final competition day
Field
150+ competitors

Challenge

Build a reliable competition robot that could perform under time, material, fabrication, and match constraints. The project was not just a CAD exercise: the design had to survive fabrication tolerances, assembly realities, and repeated testing.

Constraints

  • Course competition environment with a field of more than 150 students.
  • Mechanisms had to be manufacturable with available shop processes.
  • Design decisions had to be tested and iterated quickly.

Design Approach

The supported project record points to a design-build-test loop: develop mechanisms, fabricate parts, assemble the system, test under realistic constraints, and revise for reliability.
