MIT 16.522 - Electric Propulsion

Ion Thruster and Test Stand

Mechanical hardware and supporting test stand work for propulsion experiments, combining CAD, fabrication, assembly, instrumentation context, and plasma test evidence.

Domain
Electric propulsion
Role
Hardware and test engineering
Methods
Fabrication, instrumentation, experimental testing
Media
CAD, assembly images, plasma test video

Challenge

Build mechanical hardware and a test setup for electric propulsion experiments where assembly, alignment, instrumentation, and vacuum-compatible test behavior matter.

Constraints

  • Hardware needed to support experimental validation rather than only visual completion.
  • Mechanical assembly had to accommodate electrodes, mounting, and test stand integration.
  • Test evidence needed to show the device operating under plasma conditions.

Design Approach

The available media shows the progression from CAD to assembled hardware to plasma test evidence. The strongest next content would be a concise description of the electrode layout, mounting decisions, and instrumentation stack.

Key Decisions

Add confirmed mechanical decisions, materials, mounting strategy, instrumentation, and test constraints.

Fabrication

Repository media supports hands-on assembly and integration of the thruster hardware with a test stand plate.

Testing

The plasma images and video provide operating evidence. Add measured outputs, test conditions, and instrumentation details when available.

Improve Next

Add a diagram of the test stand, labeled CAD section, and measured test data to make the validation story complete.
