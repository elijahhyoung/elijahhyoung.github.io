MIT 16.522 - Electric Propulsion

Ion Thruster and Test Stand

Mechanical hardware and supporting test stand work for propulsion experiments, combining CAD, fabrication, assembly, instrumentation context, and plasma test evidence.

Domain
Electric propulsion
Role
Hardware and test engineering
Methods
Fabrication, instrumentation, experimental testing
Media
CAD, assembly images, plasma test video

Challenge

Build ion thruster hardware and a test setup where alignment and instrumentation actually matter, not just a CAD model that looks right and does nothing.

Constraints

  • The hardware had to actually run and produce measurable results, not just assemble cleanly.
  • Mechanical assembly had to fit electrodes, mounting, and the test stand together with no room to fudge alignment.
  • The whole point was getting plasma, so the test setup had to prove the device was operating, not just powered on.
